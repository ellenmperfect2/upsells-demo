Before we build, it helps to understand the billing pattern we're working with. A lot of AI companies — including Claude Code, Cursor, and others — use a limited usage model with the option to purchase more. Users get a credit allotment on their plan, and when they run out, they can choose to buy extra usage rather than hitting a hard wall.

Why this model works
🛡️ Prevents abuse — users have a defined budget, not unlimited access, which protects your cost of goods
🔓 Keeps options open — instead of a hard cutoff, users can decide to pay for more and keep going
🤝 Puts teams in control — admins can evaluate case by case whether extra spend is worth it, or let the limit stand
📈 Encourages usage growth — there's no fear of wasted plan spend, and power users can always expand without switching plans
